MRun Men are National Champions!


Men take 1st place at NIRCA Cross Country National Championships

On Saturday, November 14th, members of the Michigan Running Club put on their spikes for the last cross country meet of the season- the NIRCA Cross Country National Championships. Collegiate teams from all across the country traveled to the event, which was held in Bloomington, Indiana at Indiana University. Going into the race, the MRun men were only ranked 8th in the country, but they were finally able to showcase all of their hard-work and talent at Nationals. Not only did the men take 1st place out of 35 teams, but also had three runners in the top 10, including Duriel Hardy, who won the individual race. The women’s team finished in 10th place. Mollie Pozolo make first team all-American, with an impressive 11th place finish.

Hardy finished the men’s 8K race in 25:54, 20 seconds ahead of the 2nd place finisher. The 3rd male to cross the finish line, also from MRun, was senior Nathan Peters, with a time of 26:00. This was Peters 4th year in the top 10 at NIRCA National Championships. The 3rd male from the Michigan Running Club to make first team all-American was Max Hock (10th place, 26:39). Finishing close behind Hock and rounding out the scorers for the men’s team were Joseph Billian (17th place, 26:56) and Alex Prasad (32nd place, 27:13).

Mollie Pozolo, finished the women’s 6K in 22:57, only 2 seconds behind the 10th place finisher. Despite missing some of their top-finishers from previous meets of the season, the ladies’ team still finished strong as individual women stepped up to the plate. The second MRunner to cross the finish line was Whitney Gorris, with at time of 25:49. Following right behind her, at 25:52, was Katie Grassa. Soon after Grassa, Lindsay Doherty (26:14) and Melanie Jackson (26:16) finished within seconds of each other.

This is the first time that the Michigan Running Club’s men’s team has been National Champions. This is also the first time that the club has had 4 individuals make the all-American first team. The race was a great way to end the season and show the rest of the country how hard MRun has been training all semester. After some well-deserved time off, MRun will begin its track season that will continue throughout the winter and spring.
